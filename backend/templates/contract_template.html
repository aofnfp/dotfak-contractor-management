8. Equipment and Devices

The Contractor shall be solely responsible for all equipment, devices, and tools provided or made available for the performance of services under this Agreement. The Contractor shall:

  • Maintain all equipment in good working condition;
  • Use equipment solely for authorized business purposes;
  • Return all Company and Client equipment within forty-eight (48) hours of termination of this Agreement, regardless of the reason for termination;
  • Bear financial responsibility for any loss, damage, or failure to return equipment.

Failure to return equipment within the specified timeframe may result in deduction from the Contractor's final payment or other remedies available to the Company.

9. Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure

The Contractor acknowledges that during the course of this engagement, they may have access to confidential and proprietary information belonging to the Company, the Client, or their respective affiliates ("Confidential Information"). The Contractor agrees to:

  • Not disclose, share, or otherwise make available any Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent;
  • Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing services under this Agreement;
  • Take all reasonable measures to protect the confidentiality of such information;
  • Return or destroy all Confidential Information upon termination of this Agreement.

This confidentiality obligation shall survive the termination of this Agreement for a period of two (2) years.

10. Data Privacy and Security

The Contractor shall handle all data in accordance with applicable privacy and data protection laws. The Contractor agrees to:

  • Not store Client or Company data on personal devices beyond what is strictly necessary for the performance of services;
  • Use secure and encrypted methods for data transmission and storage;
  • Report any data breach, security incident, or suspected unauthorized access to the Company within twenty-four (24) hours of discovery;
  • Comply with all data handling policies provided by the Company or Client.

11. Workspace Requirements

The Contractor shall perform all services from a private and secure location that is suitable for handling confidential business information. The Contractor specifically agrees to:

  • Work from a dedicated workspace that provides visual and auditory privacy;
  • Not perform services from public spaces such as cafes, libraries, or open co-working areas without private rooms;
  • Ensure that screens displaying Client or Company information are not visible to unauthorized individuals;
  • Use a privacy screen on all work devices when applicable.

12. Intellectual Property

All work product, deliverables, inventions, documents, software, designs, and other materials created by the Contractor in the course of performing services under this Agreement ("Work Product") shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company and/or the Client, as applicable. The Contractor hereby assigns all rights, title, and interest in such Work Product to the Company.

13. Non-Solicitation

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months following its termination, the Contractor shall not, directly or indirectly:

  • Solicit, recruit, or attempt to hire any contractor, employee, or consultant engaged by the Company;
  • Solicit or attempt to divert business from any client of the Company with whom the Contractor had contact during the term of this Agreement.

14. Independent Contractor Status

The Contractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of the Company. The Contractor shall be solely responsible for:

  • All tax obligations in their jurisdiction of residence, including income tax, self-employment tax, and any applicable withholdings;
  • Obtaining and maintaining any required business licenses or permits;
  • Their own health insurance, retirement savings, and other benefits.

The Company shall not withhold taxes, provide employee benefits, or make contributions to social security, unemployment insurance, or workers' compensation on behalf of the Contractor.

15. Liability and Indemnification

The Contractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Company, its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, and expenses (including reasonable attorney's fees) arising out of or related to:

  • The Contractor's breach of any term of this Agreement;
  • The Contractor's negligence or willful misconduct;
  • Any claim by a third party related to the Contractor's performance of services.

16. Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement by providing thirty (30) days written notice to the other Party. The Company reserves the right to terminate this Agreement immediately and without notice for cause, including but not limited to:

  • Material breach of any provision of this Agreement;
  • Gross misconduct or insubordination;
  • Substantiated complaints from the Client regarding the Contractor's performance or behavior;
  • Violation of confidentiality, data privacy, or security obligations;
  • Failure to return equipment within the specified timeframe.

Upon termination, the Contractor shall:

  • Immediately cease all work and return all equipment within forty-eight (48) hours;
  • Return or destroy all Confidential Information in their possession;
  • Provide final invoicing for approved and completed work.

Final payment shall be processed in accordance with Section 7, less any deductions for unreturned equipment or other amounts owed to the Company.
